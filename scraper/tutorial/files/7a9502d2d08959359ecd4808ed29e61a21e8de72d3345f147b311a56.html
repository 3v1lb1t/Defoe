It’s no secret that Bruneians love their food. The country is packed with eateries providing cuisine of every kind so if you’re feeling hungry, there is an enormous choice of restaurants to choose from! From local cuisine to continental fare, there is something for everyone.



prev

next

AMBUYAT

If there is any dish that is uniquely Bruneian, it would be ambuyat - a thick, white, starch-like mixture with a tenacious consistency and eaten with a spicy sauce.

ROTI KUNING

Roti Kuning - toasted fresh bun with a slice of cold butter and the restaurant’s signature kaya jam – at Brunei’s oldest and most well-known coffee shop.

ROJAK

KK Koya is famous for its legendary rojak – arguably the best dish of rojak served in the whole country.

PULUT PANGGANG

Pulut panggang – glutinous rice soaked in coconut milk with a spicy dried shrimp filling wrapped in a banana leaf and grilled over a charcoal fire. A true local delight!
